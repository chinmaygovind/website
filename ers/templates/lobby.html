How to play

The whole deck is dealt out face-down between everyone and you never look at your cards. It is a game of speed: watch the pile in the middle and be the fastest to slap it when it counts.

Your turn

When it is your turn, flip your top card face-up onto the pile in the middle (press F, or tap your pile). Play then moves on to the next player.

Slapping

Any player can slap the pile at any moment (press Space, or hit the big SLAP button). If the pile is slappable right then, the first person to slap wins the whole pile and adds it to the bottom of their stack. The pile is slappable when:

  • Double - the top two cards are the same rank, like a 7 landing on a 7.
  • Sandwich - the top card matches the card two back, with one different card between them (a 7, then a 4, then a 7).
  • Top and bottom - the card on top matches the very bottom card of the pile.
  • Adds to ten - the top two cards total exactly ten, like a 6 and a 4. An Ace counts as one, and Jacks, Queens and Kings do not count.
  • Marriage - a King and a Queen are played one right after the other, in either order.

Face cards

Play a Jack, Queen, King or Ace and the next player owes a tribute of 1, 2, 3 or 4 cards. They flip that many, hoping to land a face card of their own. If they do, the tribute passes on to the player after them. If they run out of tries first, whoever played the last face card scoops up the entire pile.

Careful

Slap when the pile is not slappable and you burn two of your own cards to the bottom of the pile, and you cannot slap again until the next card is played. Slap fast, but slap smart.

Winning

Running out of cards does not knock you out right away, you can still slap back in on the next slappable pile. The last player left holding the entire deck wins the game.
